Structural Analysis

Structural analysis is a fundamental branch of civil engineering that involves determining the effects of loads on physical structures and their components. It ensures structures like buildings, bridges, towers, and dams remain safe, stable, and efficient under various conditions.

It helps engineers predict how structures will respond to forces like gravity, wind, seismic activity, and temperature changes‚Äîessential for safe design and construction.

Methods of Structural Analysis

Analytical Methods

  • Static Analysis: For structures under constant load.
  • Dynamic Analysis: For time-varying forces (e.g., earthquakes).
  • Linear Analysis: Assumes small deformations and linear behavior.
  • Non-linear Analysis: For large deformations or complex behavior.

Classical Methods

  • Moment Distribution Method
  • Slope-Deflection Method
  • Method of Joints and Sections (for trusses)

Matrix Methods & Software Tools

Modern analysis uses tools such as:

  • STAAD.Pro
  • ETABS
  • SAP2000
  • ANSYS

These tools use Finite Element Method (FEM) for accurate results on complex structures.
