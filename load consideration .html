Load Consideration

Load consideration is a fundamental aspect of civil engineering and structural design. It refers to the analysis and application of various forces or loads that a structure is expected to experience during its lifespan. Accurate load consideration ensures the safety, stability, and durability of structures like buildings, bridges, towers, and dams.

Dead Load

These are the permanent static loads that remain constant throughout the life of the structure. Dead loads include the weight of structural components such as beams, columns, slabs, roofing, walls, and finishes.

Live Load

These are temporary or movable loads that change with time. Examples include people, furniture, vehicles, and temporary equipment.

Environmental Loads

  • Wind Load: Acts horizontally and affects tall structures like towers and skyscrapers.
  • Snow Load: The weight of accumulated snow on roofs in cold regions.
  • Earthquake Load: Vibrations and shaking during an earthquake.
  • Rain Load: Collected rainwater adds pressure on roofs if not properly drained.

Impact Load

Sudden or short-duration loads, such as vehicles moving over a bridge or machinery dropping on a floor.

Thermal Load

Expansion and contraction due to temperature changes can cause stress in materials.

Settlement Load

Caused by uneven settlement of the foundation soil beneath the structure.

Hydrostatic and Soil Pressure

Structures like basements, dams, and retaining walls experience pressure from water or soil.
